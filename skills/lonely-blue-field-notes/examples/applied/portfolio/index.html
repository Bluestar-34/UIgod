practice

Design direction for people who live inside tools all day.

Working method

I focus on systems where the interface has to carry thought, not just decoration. The goal is usually to make dense work calmer: incident response, note retrieval, operational publishing, or research pipelines that accumulate too much noise too quickly.

My bias is toward editorial structure, legible hierarchy, and interfaces that feel authored rather than factory-made. When something is high stakes, I would rather reduce friction and improve recall than chase novelty for its own sake.
